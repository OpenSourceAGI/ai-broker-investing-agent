Copy Trading on Polymarket: Outsource Alpha to Proven Wallets

Polymarket has a property most trading venues don't: it's fully on-chain. Every position, every fill, every wallet's running P&L is public on Polygon. That means the wallets that are consistently profitable aren't a secret — you can find them, and you can mirror them.

That's the entire premise of Polymarket copy trading: instead of researching markets yourself, you track wallets that have already proven they have an edge, and you copy their BUY/SELL actions automatically with your own sizing.

Why copy trading works especially well here

On a centralized exchange, copy trading depends on someone opting in to publish their trades, usually with a delay and a marketing incentive to look better than they are. On Polymarket, the data is the chain. There's no opt-in, no survivorship-biased leaderboard, no spin — you can audit a wallet's real history before you decide to follow it.

That makes wallet selection a genuine, verifiable edge: you're choosing demonstrated performance, not a curated highlight reel.

How a copy-trading bot works

  1. Track one or more target wallets. You pick the wallets to mirror based on their on-chain track record.
  2. Detect their actions in near-real time. The bot watches for the target's BUY/SELL activity as it lands.
  3. Mirror with your own sizing. Instead of copying their dollar amounts, you copy their decisions at a configurable percentage — e.g. 10% of every position they take — so the strategy scales to your bankroll.
  4. Apply your own risk limits. A circuit breaker and depth checks sit between the signal and your order, so a target's bad day or a thin market doesn't wreck you.

Zero research, zero chart-watching, zero second-guessing. The bot copies; you compound.

The things that actually matter

  • Wallet selection is everything. Copy a wallet that got lucky and you inherit variance, not edge. Audit the on-chain history — consistency over time beats one big score.
  • Speed of mirroring. The later you copy, the worse your entry price versus the wallet you're following. Near-real-time detection keeps your fills close to theirs.
  • Sizing as a percentage, not a copy. Mirroring raw dollar amounts blows up small accounts and underweights large ones. Copy the decision at your size.
  • Multi-wallet diversification. Following several proven wallets smooths the variance of any single one having a bad week.
